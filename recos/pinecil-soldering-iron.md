# Pinecil Soldering Iron

{:.lead}
A portable, USB-C powered soldering iron with precision temperature control and open-source firmware — perfect for bench work and field repairs.

![Pinecil Soldering Iron]({{ '/images/recos/pinecil.jpg' | relative_url }}){:.hero-image}

## Why It's Great

The Pinecil has become my go-to soldering iron for almost every project. Here's why it stands out:

- **USB-C Power Delivery** — Works with any 65W+ USB-C PD charger or power bank. No proprietary adapters, no bulky power supply.
- **Rapid Heat-Up** — Reaches 300°C in under 12 seconds. No more waiting around.
- **Precision Control** — Adjustable temperature from 100°C to 400°C with ±1°C stability. Perfect for delicate SMD work and heavy ground planes alike.
- **Open-Source Firmware** — Runs IronOS (formerly Ralim's firmware). Fully customizable, with active community development and regular updates.
- **Portable & Lightweight** — Small form factor fits in any toolbox. Great for field repairs, maker events, or traveling to hackathons.
- **TS100/TS80 Compatible Tips** — Wide range of tip options from multiple vendors. Easy to find replacements.
- **OLED Display** — Shows real-time temperature, power draw, and settings. Clear feedback at a glance.
- **Affordable** — Around $25-35 USD. Incredible value for the feature set.

## Use Cases

Perfect for:
- SMD soldering and rework (0402, 0603, QFP, SOIC packages)
- Through-hole component assembly
- Wire splicing and connector work
- Portable field repairs
- Electronics workshops and maker events
- Learning to solder (great first iron)

Not ideal for:
- Very large ground planes (may need more thermal mass)
- Extended heavy-duty production runs (designed for hobby/repair use)
