# Wireless Dremel Rotary Tool

{:.lead}
A cordless rotary tool for cutting, drilling, grinding, and finishing 3D-printed enclosures and PCB work — freedom from power cords makes all the difference.

![Wireless Dremel Rotary Tool]({{ '/images/recos/wireless-dremel.jpg' | relative_url }}){:.hero-image}

## Why It's Essential

Going cordless was a game-changer for enclosure work. Here's why a wireless rotary tool belongs in every maker's toolkit:

- **Cordless Freedom** — No power cord getting tangled in your work. Move freely around your bench or take it to the project.
- **Precision Cutting** — Perfect for cutting holes in 3D-printed enclosures for displays, switches, and connectors.
- **Versatile Tool System** — One tool, dozens of attachments: cutting discs, grinding wheels, drill bits, sanding drums, polishing pads.
- **Speed Control** — Variable speed (5,000 to 30,000+ RPM) for different materials. Slow for delicate work, fast for cutting.
- **Battery Runtime** — Lithium-ion batteries provide 30-60 minutes of continuous use. Swap batteries for longer sessions.
- **Safe for Electronics** — Unlike a full-size angle grinder, it's gentle enough for PCB work and fine adjustments.
- **Quick Cleanup** — Grind away 3D-print support material, smooth rough edges, or deburr drilled holes in seconds.

**Electronics:**

- Polishing acrylic windows
- Removing excess epoxy
- Detail work on custom enclosures

## Essential Accessories

**Must-have bits:**
- **Reinforced cut-off discs** — For cutting plastic, thin metal, and PCBs
- **Small drill bits** (0.5-3mm) — For mounting holes and cable pass-throughs
- **Sanding drums** — Various grits for smoothing 3D-printed surfaces
- **Grinding stones** — For shaping and deburring
- **Polishing wheels** — For finishing acrylic and metal

**Pro tips:**
- Keep a variety pack of cutting discs on hand (they break easily)
- Use lower speeds for plastic to avoid melting
- Wear safety glasses — bits can break and send debris flying
- Work over a drop cloth — dust and debris go everywhere

## Where to Buy

Available from:
- **Dremel brand** — Original, high quality, widely available
- **Bosch** — Excellent alternative with compatible accessories
- **Amazon Basics** — Budget option, decent for occasional use
- Hardware stores (Home Depot, Lowe's, etc.)

**Pro tip:** Look for combo kits that include a variety of bits and accessories. More cost-effective than buying individually.
