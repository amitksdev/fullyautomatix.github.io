A running list of creators, educators, and blogs that have shaped many of these hobby electronics and 3D-printing automation ideas.

  • GreatScott!: Practical electronics explanations, test equipment usage, and clear project walk-throughs.

  • DroneBot Workshop: Breadboard-to-firmware tutorials that are ideal for hobby prototyping and learning-by-doing.
  • Adafruit Learning System: Structured project guides, code examples, and troubleshooting notes for all skill levels.
  • SparkFun Blog: Build ideas, product teardowns, and approachable tutorials for sensors, comms, and embedded gadgets.
  • Hackaday: Daily inspiration from creative hardware hacks, reverse engineering, and custom fabrication.

Component Sourcing

Where to Buy Electronics Parts

Use this section to keep your go-to vendors for sensors, passives, modules, connectors, and prototyping hardware. These links are easy to swap with your preferred sources.

  • DigiKey: Excellent stock depth, fast shipping, and strong parametric search for exact components.
  • Mouser: Great for broad manufacturer coverage, datasheet access, and engineering-grade parts.
  • LCSC: Very cost-effective for hobby and small-batch builds with wide module and component variety.
  • Adafruit: Best for beginner-friendly breakout boards, clear documentation, and reliable tutorials.
  • SparkFun: Strong prototyping ecosystem with useful dev boards, shields, and educational resources.
